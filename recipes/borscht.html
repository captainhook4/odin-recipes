Borscht

Description

"Georgia O’Keeffe loved innovating in the kitchen and was known for her healthy dishes made with colorful ingredients—unsurprising, given her fondness for color in her paintings. For the 2017 book Dinner with Georgia O’Keeffe, published by Assouline, writer Robyn Lea dug through the artist’s notes to compile this cookbook, inspired by O’Keeffe’s favorite dishes that she either cooked herself or had served to her by her caretakers. One of the most colorful dishes in the book: Brightest Borscht with Sour Cream & Fresh Dill."

Ingredients

  • 4 large beets
  • 1 tsp salt, plus more to taste
  • 1 tbsp suger
  • 3 egg yolks, lightly beaten
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • 4-5 tbsp sour cream, plus more for garnish
  • A few sprigs chopped dill, for garnish
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • (Optional) Hard boiled eggs, for garnish

Steps

  1. Trim and wash the beets, leaving the skin on, then olace them in a large saucepan and cover them with about 2½ quarts of water
  2. Add the 1 teaspoon of salt
  3. Cover the pot and simmer for about 1 hour, or until tender when pierced with a knife
  4. Remove the beets with a large slotted spoon and place them in a sieve to drain
  5. Reserve 1 ½ quarts of the cooking liquid
  6. Allow the beets to cool, then peel and grate them
  7. In a small saucepan, heat 1 cup of the cooking liquid, then add the sugar and stir to dissolve
  8. Slowly drizzle the hot liquid into the egg yolks, whisking to combine, then stir in the lemon juice
  9. Pour the egg mixture into a large bowl and add the remaining cooking liquid and grated beets
  10. Add the sour cream, then season with salt and pepper, stirring to combine well
  11. Chill until cold, about 2 hours
  12. Garnish with dill, pepper, and more sour cream
